Project 1

Speech Emotion Recognition

Speech emotion recognition (SER) is a technology that uses machine learning to identify the emotions of a speaker from their voice. It is a challenging task, as human emotions are complex and can be expressed in many different ways. However, SER has the potential to revolutionize the way we interact with computers and each other. One of the most popular datasets used for SER research is the RAVDESS dataset. This dataset contains over 7,000 audio recordings of 24 actors speaking eight different emotions (neutral, happy, sad, angry, fearful, surprised, disgusted, and calm).
